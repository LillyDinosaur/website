Goal
Create a personal website for a 13‑year‑old girl named Casey, designed for a young adult / teen audience.
The output should be a series of html pages with css and vanilla JavaScript

Implementation progress
- [x] Chosen a bold pink-and-tropical scrapbook direction
- [x] Built a multi-page static site scaffold in HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript
- [x] Added shared navigation, responsive layouts, and light interactivity
- [ ] Final review and polish

Context
The website is for Casey (age 13) and should feel:

Fun, expressive, and age‑appropriate
“Young adult” rather than childish
Friendly, upbeat, and personality‑driven

Casey’s Interests (must strongly influence design & content)

📚 Books
🏒 Hockey
👯 Her friends
🧁 Baking
📺 “Trashy” / guilty‑pleasure TV shows
🐶🐱 Pets:

Milo (dog)
Ruby (cat)
Quin (cat)



Visual & Styling Inspiration

Bright pinks as the primary color
Soft light oranges / coral tones as accents
Sea shells, palm trees, beachy / tropical elements
Overall vibe: summer, fun, cute but cool, confident


Target Audience

Primary: Teens / young adults (ages ~12–18)
Secondary: Friends, family, classmates

Tone should be:

Positive, upbeat, playful
Never patronizing or babyish
Safe, wholesome, and age‑appropriate


Deliverables Required
1. Product Specification (PRD‑style)
Include the following sections:
a. Purpose & Vision

What this site represents for Casey
How it reflects her personality and interests
How it should make visitors feel

b. Core Goals

Self‑expression
Showcasing hobbies and interests
A fun digital “home” for friends to explore
Light, joyful browsing (not serious or corporate)

c. Sitemap / Page List
Provide a recommended set of pages, such as:

Home
About Casey
Hobbies & Interests (Books, Hockey, Baking, TV)
Friends / Memories
Pets (Milo, Ruby, Quin)
Gallery or Scrapbook
Optional extras (Blog, Favorites, Mood Board, etc.)

Explain why each page exists.
d. Content Strategy
For each major page:

What type of content lives there
Text tone and voice
Image ideas (photos, illustrations, icons)
How her interests should be featured (not generic filler)


2. Design Brief
a. Visual Identity

Color palette (describe specific pinks, corals, oranges)
Use of white space and softness
Rounded vs sharp elements
Overall aesthetic (e.g., beachy, scrapbook, lifestyle)

b. Typography

Font personality (playful, modern, friendly)
Heading vs body text guidance
Readability for teen audiences

c. Imagery & Iconography

How to incorporate:

Seashells
Palm trees
Stickers, doodles, or scrapbook‑style accents


Pet imagery ideas
Hockey & baking visuals without feeling cluttered

d. Layout & UI Style

Page structure descriptions (hero sections, cards, grids)
Button styles
Hover or interaction ideas (conceptual only)
Mobile‑first considerations


3. Page‑by‑Page Wireframe Descriptions (Text Only)
For each main page:

Top section (hero)
Middle content sections
Callouts or highlights
Fun, personality‑driven elements (quotes, badges, favorites)

No drawings required — written descriptions only.

4. Do’s and Don’ts
Do:

Lean into pink and fun
Highlight pets and hobbies
Keep everything age‑appropriate
Make the site feel personal and expressive

Don’t:

Over‑sexualize or mature the design
Make it childish or cartoonish
Overload pages with clutter
Use dark or moody tones


5. Success Criteria
The plan is successful if:

A developer could build the site from this document alone
Casey’s personality is clearly reflected
The site feels fun, modern, and confident
The design aligns strongly with pink + tropical inspiration
