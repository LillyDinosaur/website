Hobbies & interests

Books, hockey, baking, and shows that are just a little bit chaotic.

The hobbies page should feel like Casey’s favorite mix of cozy, sporty, and fun. Each section gets enough space to breathe so nothing feels crowded or generic.

📚 reads fast 🏒 plays hard 🧁 bakes often 📺 watches for fun
Books

Reading that feels like a reward.

Give this section energetic book cover tiles, favorite genres, and a short list of what Casey likes in a story: strong characters, fast pacing, and enough drama to keep it interesting.

  • Favorites can be displayed as cover-style cards.
  • Include a “currently reading” line on rotation.
  • Use quote callouts for memorable lines.
Hockey

Competitive energy, but still fun.

This page should celebrate movement, teamwork, and confidence. Use puck-like buttons, score-card accents, and sporty shapes without making the layout feel heavy or aggressive.

  • Show team spirit, favorite position, or game-day rituals.
  • Add stats-style cards with playful wording.
  • Keep the tone enthusiastic and proud.
Baking

Soft, sweet, and always a little aesthetic.

Baking content should look delicious and cheerful. Think recipe cards, frosting swirls, and notes about the treats Casey likes making for friends and family.

  • Use recipe-card layouts and ingredient tags.
  • Include favorite flavors or decorating styles.
  • Mix in hand-drawn doodles like stars and hearts.
TV

Guilty-pleasure shows only.

Keep the TV section fun and slightly dramatic, with bright episode cards and cheeky labels. This is for low-stakes, high-entertainment shows that are easy to binge.

  • Group by mood: funny, messy, or comfort-watch.
  • Use rounded cards that feel like streaming thumbnails.
  • Write with humor, not sarcasm.
